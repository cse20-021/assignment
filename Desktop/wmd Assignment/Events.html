Night life and Entertainment

La Geode
Location: Parc de la Villette, 26 avenue Corentin Cariou, 75019 Paris
Opening hours: Tue - Thur: 10:30 a.m. - 6:30 p.m.; Fri - Sun: 10:30 a.m. - 8:30 p.m.(timings vary on Monday)
Entry fee: Everyone - €10.50; Below the age of 25 and above 60 - €9 for people; Not suitable for kids below the age of 3. Not advised for women who are more than 6 months pregnant
Description: La Geode is a special cinema hall that can accommodate 400 people at any given time. The exteriors are in the form of a giant mirror-finished steel globe which is quite impressive. You will love watching movies on its 1000 square metre hemispherical screen. It uses an Omnimax projector that produces an image nine times larger than that of an ordinary projector. A great experience altogether, it is definitely a must-do on your trip to Paris.

Moulin Rouge
Location: 82 Boulevard de Clichy, 75018 Paris
Opening hours: Dinner with show at 7:00 p.m.: 1st show - 9:00 p.m.; 2nd show - 11:00 p.m.
Website: www.moulinrouge.com
Description: Paris has an enthralling nightlife, which is best experienced in and around the Moulin Rouge area. The Moulin Rouge cabaret show is a glitzy, glamorous affair of fancy costumes, enthralling dances and mesmerizing music. Ever since it opened in 1889, Moulin Rouge has raised the bar for cabaret performances across the world. You can enjoy a sumptuous dinner with champagne as you watch the show if you opt for the wine and dine package. Don't forget to take a photo souvenir of your Moulin Rouge experience on your way back. Prior reservations are recommended.

Rue de la Huchette
Location: Rue de la Huchette, 75005 Paris, France (Panthéon)
Opening hours: Varies from restaurant to restaurant
Description: One of the oldest streets in Paris, Rue de la Huchette is now famous for having the highest concentration of restaurants in the whole city. The Greek restaurants here are particularly popular with tourists. If you are a foodie, you will definitely love this place and will be spoilt for choice. Open till late hours, you can experience the nightlife of Paris at the bars and pubs of Rue de la Huchette. A pedestrian friendly, tourist and vibrant street, you can enjoy food, drinks or coffee at one of the many restaurants here.

Sporting events

Want to talk sports?? You have come to the right city. Parisians are very big fans of sports be it tennis, cycling, judo, athletics you name it but the most practiced sport would have to be football.Below are examples of sports and events that the Parisians find delight in participating in.

Football
The parisisians are bog fans of football,hence the fomration of the 3 professional clubs being the Red Star FC, Paris FC and the famous Paris Saint-Germain(PSG) respectively. The PSG was found by Henri Partelle and was launched on the 12th of August 1970 Of the many football stadium in the city of lights, the Stade de France is the largest in France with a holding capacity of 80,698.The Stade is known for hosting estimable events such as the final games of the Champions Legue, European Championship and the world cup.

Cycling
Tour de France
It's the moment all cycling fans have been waiting for, the final stage of the Tour de France, and the finishing line on the Avenue des Champs-Elysées. After more than three weeks of racing, the racers can finally push for home and celebrate the winner of the coveted yellow jersey. From Place de la Concorde, to the Champs-Elysées and along the Quai des Tuileries, you can cheer on the riders at several places along the route. Although this final stint usually favours sprinters, the rest of the pack will be hoping to confound the forecasts. One thing is certain, the yellow jersey will remain the property of the leader of the Tour, who will celebrate his title on the Champs-Elysées after crossing the Tour de France finishing line

Musical events

La Défense Jazz Festival
The forecourt at the Ile-de-France business district is transformed into an open-air concert hall on the occasion of its famous jazz festival. For 40 years now, prestigious artists such as Stacey Kent, Erik Truffaz, andLionel Belmondo have performed here and this year’s line-up has many beautiful moments in store, with notably the participation of Bootsy Collins, Chinese Man, Alsarah & The Nubatones or ALA.NI. Free and quality entertainment to listen to at lunchtime or in the evening

Deep Purple
Hard rock fans have something to celebrate! Deep Purple will be in concert at the Seine Musicale. The legendary British band will electrify the stage, performing the mythical songs that have forged their identity and legend for more than 50 years.This events usually takes place at around 8pm CET at a price that starts from 63 euros.

Food events

Salon du Chocolat
The Salon du Chocolat is a festival centered around the world of, you guessed it, chocolate! Each year, thousands of visitors flock to the Parc des Expositions in Paris to peruse the stands of the 280 exhibitors present. Visitors will find chocolatiers, patissiers, confectioners, chocolate experts, cocoa producing countries and other artisans from all over the world. In addition to delicious samples, there are many other activities to do such as a pastry show, and cocoa show, and even a chocolate bookshop. There are also awards given to the best of the best present at the festival. For all of you chocolate lovers out there, this is a must-see.

Bouche á bouche
Literally meaning ‘From Mouth to Mouth’, Bouche à Bouche is a food festival that also features excellent music. The idea behind the festival is to listen to DJ sets, while eating delicious food. The event features live performances and the best part is, entry is free, and plates start at €7,50. The program includes food workshops with renowned chefs and an ample choice of beverages with wine, beer, champagne, and also delicious coffee
